Mark Lanegan

The voice of a generation

Singer and writer, Mark Lanegan (November 25, 1964 – February 22, 2022). The main brain behind Screaming Trees and his own solo works.

A little bit about Lanegan's life:

  • 1964, November 25: Mark William Lanegan is born in Ellensburg, Washington, USA.
  • 1980s, Early Years and Musical Beginnings: Lanegan begins his musical career as a teenager, initially influenced by punk and rock music. He forms the band Screaming Trees in 1985, a group that would become a significant part of the grunge and alternative rock scenes of the 1990s.
  • 1990s, Breakthrough with Screaming Trees: Screaming Trees release their major label debut album, Sweet Oblivion, in 1992. The album is well-received, particularly for the hit single “Nearly Lost You”. Lanegan also begins a side career as a solo artist. His first solo album, The Winding Sheet, is released in 1990, showcasing his deep, distinctive voice and more melancholic musical style.
  • 1994: Lanegan collaborates with Kurt Cobain and other members of Nirvana on the song "Where Did You Sleep Last Night" (a traditional folk song), which appears on the MTV Unplugged in New York album.
  • 1998: Lanegan forms a new supergroup, Queens of the Stone Age, alongside Josh Homme. He contributes vocals to their self-titled debut album, marking a major part of his career.
  • 2001: Lanegan releases Bubblegum, his fourth solo album, receiving critical acclaim.
  • 2004: He collaborates with Greg Dulli of The Afghan Whigs to form the band The Gutter Twins, releasing Saturnalia in 2008.
  • 2012: Lanegan releases Blues Funeral, which continues his exploration of dark rock, blues, and alternative genres. Lanegan's writing also gains attention in the 2010s, with the release of his memoir, Sing Backwards and Weep, which is published in 2020.
  • 2017: He releases Gargoyle, his 10th studio album, further cementing his reputation as a master of atmospheric, brooding rock.
  • 2020: Lanegan is diagnosed with COVID-19, which leaves him seriously ill. He later reflects on his battle with the virus in interviews.
  • 2022: Lanegan dies from complications related to COVID-19 on February 22, 2022, at the age of 57. His death is mourned by fans and fellow musicians worldwide.

    • Mark Lanegan’s legacy as a powerful voice in alternative rock, with his signature gravelly tones and his deeply emotional, often haunting lyrics, continues to influence generations of musicians.
